Flight Dispatch

Builds the flight plan with routes, fuel, alternates, and performance limits.

Maintenance Control

Guides troubleshooting and ensures aircraft are airworthy before release.

Line Maintenance

Hands‑on techs who fix squawks, change tires, and clear MELs at the gate.

Meteorology

Analyzes METARs/TAFs, turbulence forecasts, and convective weather.

Operations Control (AOC)

Monitors the whole network; coordinates disruptions and swaps aircraft.

Load Control

Computes weight & balance, sets CG limits, and produces the final load sheet.

Baggage Team

Sorts, scans, and loads bags to the correct hold positions for balance.

Ramp Agents

Marshalling, chocking, cones, loaders, and pushback coordination.

Fuelers

Refuel to planned kilos/pounds; verify quality and bonding/grounding.

De‑icing Crew

Removes contamination and protects surfaces with Type I/IV fluids.

Gate Agents

Boarding order, documents, seat changes, and special assistance.

Security

Screening, gate door controls, and ramp access compliance.

Cabin Cleaners

Quick turns: trash, lav service, galley restock, and safety card checks.

Catering

Loads meals, water, and service items; manages galley weights.

Avionics Techs

Inspect and troubleshoot navigation, radios, and flight deck systems.

Runway Safety

Prevents incursions with markings, lighting, hold‑short discipline.

FOD Walks

Foreign object debris patrols keep ramps and taxiways clear.

NOTAMs

Notices to Air Missions—temporary changes pilots and dispatchers brief.

ATIS & Clearance

Automatic weather/airfield info and IFR clearances before push.

Pushback

Towbar/towbarless tugs coordinate with the cockpit and ground control.

Marshalling

Hand signals guide precise stand entry and parking alignment.

APU & GPU

Auxiliary/ground power for electrics and air while engines are off.

Briefings

Pilots and cabin crew align on weather, alternates, MELs, and duties.

Cross‑checks

Door armed/disarmed verification; call‑and‑response to avoid error.

Sterile Cockpit

No nonessential talk below 10,000 feet to preserve focus.

TCAS & GPWS

Collision and terrain warnings with standardized pilot responses.

Alternate Planning

Fuel and legal alternates for weather, runway work, or traffic.

Time‑outs & Duty

Fatigue rules limit duty time; crews must remain legal to operate.

Safety Management

Hazard reporting and data trend analysis drive continuous improvement.

First Aid & AED

Cabin crew trained for medical events with kits and defibrillators.
